# METALLURGY ACADEMY: HIGH SCHOOL STEM CURRICULUM
## Prepared for St. Andrew High School & Jamaican Science Consortium

### 1. Introduction to Materials Science
*   **Basics of Solid Matter**: Understanding the atomic bonding of metals, ceramics, glass, plastics, and composites.
*   **Properties & Structures**: Comparative analysis of density, melting points, and hardness.

### 2. Materials Science & Engineering (Specialized)
*   **Engineering Design**: Selection of materials based on structural application and load constraints.
*   **Manufacturing Systems I**: Production and processing technologies (Forge vs. Foundry).

### 3. Physics & Chemistry of Materials
*   **Crystal Structures**: BCC, FCC, and HCP lattice models.
*   **Atomic Bonding**: Metallic vs. Covalent bonding in semiconductors and alloys.
*   **Hooke's Law**: Young's Modulus and the Stress-Strain proportionality.

### 4. Nanotechnology Modules
*   **Introduction to Nanomaterials**: Properties of matter at the 1-100nm scale.
*   **Advanced Applications**: Carbon nanotubes and quantum dot mechanics.

### 5. Career & Technical Education (CTE) - Manufacturing
*   **Welding & Joining**: Thermal fusion of similar and dissimilar metals.
*   **Carpentry**: Structural biocomposites and timber engineering.
*   **Electronics**: Solder alloys and conductive properties.
