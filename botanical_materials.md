# BOTANICAL MATERIALS & BIOCOMPOSITES
## Global and Local (Jamaican) Species Profile

### 1. Mahogany (Swietenia mahagoni) - Local Focus
*   **Growing Range**: Native to Jamaica and the Caribbean.
*   **Preferred Environment**: Humid, tropical climates; thrives in limestone-rich soils.
*   **Growth Cycle**: 25–30 years for sustainable harvest.
*   **Burn Point**: ~250°C.
*   **Industrial Use**: Heavy structural furniture, boat building, and high-durability composites.

### 2. Sugar Maple (Acer saccharum) - Global Focus
*   **Growing Range**: North America (specifically Northeastern US and Canada). 
    *   *Note: You won't find these in the Jamaican wild; they require cold winters to thrive!*
*   **Preferred Environment**: Cool, temperate climates with distinct seasons.
*   **Growth Cycle**: 30–40 years for structural grade; 100+ years for full maturity.
*   **Burn Point**: ~220°C.
*   **Industrial Use**: Flooring, musical instruments, and high-performance sports equipment.

### 3. Giant Bamboo (Dendrocalamus giganteus)
*   **Growing Range**: Pantropical; widely naturalized in Jamaica.
*   **Preferred Environment**: High rainfall, warm temperatures.
*   **Growth Cycle**: 3–5 years (The world's fastest-growing biocomposite!).
*   **Burn Point**: ~205°C.
*   **Industrial Use**: Scaffolding, sustainable textiles, and carbon-negative construction panels.

---
### Why Study Non-Local Species?
By comparing the **Maple** (temperate) with the **Mahogany** (tropical), students learn how environmental constraints (temperature, soil, rainfall) dictate the physical properties of the materials we use for engineering. 

*Sources: National Science Foundation (NSF.gov), Jamaican Ministry of Science & Technology.*
